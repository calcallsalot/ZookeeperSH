Loyalists

The Bureaucrat starts the game knowing how many pairs of Fascists there are.
The Inspector starts the game knowing that one of three specific players is a particular Agent.
The Vicar starts the game knowing three players, where exactly one of them is a Fascist.
The Surveyor, each time a third policy is enacted, publicly chooses two players and learns whether either of them is the Dictator. A Liberal registers as a Dictator to the Surveyor.
The Nun, each time a policy other than a Liberal policy is enacted, exiles a player and learns how many of their living neighbors are Fascist.
The Fisherman, once per game, may publicly choose a dead player and learns that player’s role.
The Organizer, once per game, may publicly choose a player and learns whether that player is an Agent or is learning rumors.
The Deputy, when exiling a player, publicly chooses three players and learns how many of them are Dissidents.
The Journalist, when exiling a player, publicly chooses three players and learns how many of them are Liberal.
The Monk, when exiling a player, publicly chooses two players other than themselves and learns whether those two players share the same alignment.
The Harrier, upon dying, publicly chooses a player and learns that player’s role.
The Pacifist, upon dying, remains alive and publicly chooses a player, causing that player to die.
The Governor causes their team to immediately win if a Chancellor cannot be nominated.

Dissidents

The Usher, each deck, chooses a player other than themselves and must vote Ja if that player is nominated.
The Rumorist is known to the Dictator and believes they are a Loyalist even though they are not, and instead learns rumors.

Agents

The Insurrectionary, each deck, chooses a player who then learns rumors until a reshuffle occurs.
The Noble, each deck, chooses a player who will die if two consecutive elections fail before a reshuffle.
The Grandma, upon dying, remains alive and publicly chooses a player to die, and may register as any Liberal while their loyalty card remains Liberal.

Dictator

Hitler causes their team to immediately win if they are elected Chancellor after three Fascist policies have been enacted.

Rules and Notes

Exile allows any player not currently elected to gain an exile token, which must be discarded after a reshuffle. Exiled players may not run for office.
Rumors grant no abilities to the player receiving them and may contain arbitrary or false information provided by the Fascists.
